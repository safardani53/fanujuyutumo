    VICKY Pattison has checked in to Jason Vale’s Juicy Oasis retreat in Portugal to shape up following an indulgent Christmas.

    The I’m A Celebrity star went on of Jason’s seminars in London before jumping on a plane to stay in the retreats swanky villa.

    Posting a picture of herself holding a very healthy looking juice, Vicky told her Instagram followers: “10k hike done, a lovely smoothie for lunch and then on to a spot of body combat.

    “Awwww, Juicy Oasis is just what the doctor ordered this January!!! Can't THANKYOU enough Mr Jason Vale.”

    Vicky, 29, is a big fan of Jason’s retreats and spent seven days detoxing in Portugal last summer.

    Earlier this week, Vicky admitted she was determined to get back into shape, telling her fans: “Back to the gym tomorrow and absolutely smashing my @minivnutrition products and workout plans!!!

    “Who’s with me?!! My #JanPlan is to tone up again, lose 10 pounds, cut back on alcohol and sugar and improve my fitness levels!!!…”

    Vicky, pictured here with her boyfriend John Noble, partied hard over the festive seasonAt Jason's Juicy Oasis retreats  clients live exclusively on juices and smoothies, with the website explaining clients come to stay for a variety of reasons.

    It reads: “From simply wanting to loose some weight and kick-start a health and fitness regime, to needing a total recharge both mentally and physically.

    “The way you feel when you walk through our doors can be a world away from how you feel when you ‘float’ out the doors 7, 14 or 21 days later.”

    Last year Vicky admitted she’d gained a stone over Christmas and worked hard to get back into shape.

    Along with going to Jason’s retreat, she used her Mini V nutrition range and exercised regularly to transform her body again.

    Vicky’s previously teamed up with The Sun to give readers her top tips for getting the best out of your body, admitting her own weight-loss journey inspired her to help others.

    She said: “I know what I’m talking about because I’ve been on my own weight-loss journey.

    “I piled on the pounds eating takeaways and binging on alcohol-fuelled nights out, ballooning to a size 16.

    “But after I saw a picture of myself on a Marbella beach holiday in 2013, I was inspired to swap my unhealthy lifestyle for home-cooked meals and workouts.”

    When she first embarked on her new fitness regime, Vicky admits she became too thin and didn’t like the way it made her feel.

    She explained: “I got down to 8st 4lb but I was miserable. I had no social life and couldn’t
    enjoy being slim.

    “When I realised this, everything changed. I stopped beating myself up and realised the main thing is to be happy and healthy.”

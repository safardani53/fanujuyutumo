    It was announced that a TV adaptation of Holly's 2015 memoir Down the Rabbit Hole is now in the works, with Samara Weaving signed on to play Holly.

    When did Holly Madison date Criss Angel?

    Holly Madison and Criss Angel dated from November 2008 to February 2009.

    She detailed their relationship in her memoir Down the Rabbit Hole, where she described the affair as a whirlwind romance.

    "Seduced by this good-looking man who was seemingly frantic in his affections for me, I allowed myself to be lured in," she wrote.

    She went on to write that she felt their relationship was a media distraction from his failing Las Vegas residency show at the time, titled beLIEve.

    "And, like a magician does when wanting to guide the audience's attention away from a trick, he needed to provide a distraction, or in magician's terms, 'a misdirection,' for the media the night of the show's opening. That night, unbeknownst to me, I was the misdirection."

    Why did Holly Madison and Criss Angel break up?

    Criss and Holly's relationship began to unravel after Criss became "posessive."

    Holly said he insisted on one of his bodyguards travelling with her as she travelled coast to coast "in order to keep [her] safe," but that it was "awkward and embarrassing to have to explain to my friends why a security guard joined us for every meal."

    She described how Criss' behavior became too much to handle, writing: "Criss' explosive temper was becoming increasingly more alarming. I didn't want to go back to the mansion, Criss knew that, but it was as if he couldn't help his jealousy.

    "For all his fame, fortune, and success, Criss, to me, seemed cripplingly insecure. This was starting to feel all too familiar."

    She continued, "Hef was a master of manipulation and knew how to cripple a girl's self-esteem. Criss, on the other hand, just scared me."

    She added that during their fights, he'd "accuse me of being a slut" and say they should break up but then "freak out if I didn't try to convince him otherwise."

    Holly put her foot down after one bad fight and returned home to Portland after calling it quits with Criss. She shortly returned to Las Vegas, however, to star in Peep Show.

    "I felt like Hef was trying to sabotage me in Los Angeles by bad-mouthing me and leaving me out of Girls Next Door-related press, and now Criss was trying to banish me from Las Vegas," she wrote.

    "Sorry, boys, it's not going to be that easy."

    Eric O’Dell Biography

    Eric O’Dell (born 21 June 1990) is a professional ice hockey player from Canada. Metallurg Magnitogorsk of the Kontinental Hockey League is his current team (KHL). O’Dell was picked by the Anaheim Ducks in the second round (39th overall) in the 2008 NHL Entry Draft. He has since played for the Winnipeg Jets in the National Hockey League.

    Career

    • O’Dell played four seasons (2007-2011) of major junior hockey in the Ontario Hockey League (OHL), accumulating 100 goals and 107 assists for 207 points in 198 games (2007-2011). In 2008, O’Dell was selected to play for Club Canada in the IIHF World U18 Championships, where they won gold. In 2009–10, he was selected to represent his team in the OHL All-Star Game.
    • During the 2009–10 American Hockey League season, O’Dell made his professional debut with the Chicago Wolves. On June 1, 2010, the Atlanta Thrashers signed O’Dell to an entry-level contract. O’Dell played three seasons with the AHL’s St. John’s IceCaps after the Atlanta Thrashers moved to Winnipeg and became the Winnipeg Jets at the conclusion of the 2011-12 NHL season. O’Dell made his NHL debut in 2013–14 and scored his first NHL goal on January 11, 2014, against Curtis McElhinney of the Columbus Blue Jackets.
    • O’Dell became a free agent on July 20, 2021, when his contract with Sibir Novosibirsk ended, and signed a one-year deal with HC Dynamo Moscow to continue playing in the KHL.
    • In January 2022, O’Dell was nominated to Team Canada for the 2022 Winter Olympics.

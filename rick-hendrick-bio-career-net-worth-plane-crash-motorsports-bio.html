    Rick Hendrick Wiki-Bio | Early Career & Life

    Joseph Riddick “Rick” Hendrick III, popularly known as Rick Hendrick, is Mary and Joseph Hendrick, Jr and was born on 12 July 1949, in Warrenton, North Carolina.

    Growing up on a farm, he developed a passion for drag racing. Eventually, at 14 years, Rick fulfilled the dream by competing in a self-built 1931 Chevrolet.

    Similarly, Chrysler-Plymouth held a troubleshooting contest in which Hendrick was victorious. He was 16 at that time. Thereafter, Rick enrolled in Park View High School, located in South Hill, Virginia.

    While there, he considered switching to the profession of becoming a baseball player. Although upon graduation, Hendrick opted for a co-op study program granted by North Carolina State University and Westinghouse Electric Company.

    Rick Hendrick | Career: Racing & Owner

    Upon completion of the co-op program, Hendrick jumped into launching his business. With Mike Leith, Hendrick Automotive Group opened, which operated basically as a used-car lot.

    Later, the racer became the youngest Chevrolet dealer in the U.S., after selling his assets and purchasing a franchise in Bennettsville, South Carolina.

    Furthermore, his racing career includes 33rd place in the Winston Cup Series and 15th place the following year.

    His other racing credits include Busch Series and Craftsman Truck Series. Apart from being a racer, Hendrick also worked as a pit crew member for Flying 11.

    In 1996, Governor of California, Jim Hunt, bestowed the former racer with the Long Leaf Pine Order, one of the highest civilian honors.

    Continuing his business ventures, Rick established a drag boat racing team that set the world record highest speed achieved and won championships three times in a row.

    Similarly, Hendrick set foot in NASCAR Model Sportsman Series, now renamed to Xfinity Series, which earned a victory at Charlotte Motor Speedway.

    The Ohio native is a former football wide receiver in the National Football League finishing the career with 10,950 receiving yards and 77 touchdowns. He now appears as a college football analyst for the ESPN network.

    Prior to working as a football analyst, he also played for the Dallas Cowboys (2000-2003), Tampa Bay Buccaneers(2004-2008), New England Patriots(2009), Pittsburgh Steelers (2009),  and Washington Redskins(2010). He was drafted #8 by the Seattle Seahawks (1995-1999) in the 1995 NFL Draft.

    He played college football at Ohio State. He is the NFL's career leader in receiving yards and receiving touchdowns among players to not be selected for a Pro Bowl.

    Joey Galloway's Bio; Also Played Basketball

    Joey was born as Joseph Scott Galloway on 20 November 1971 to parents, Carolyn and James Galloway.

    He graduated high school from Bellaire High School and later went on to complete his graduation from Ohio State in 1995 with a degree in business marketing.

    From his high school days he was interested in football and during his senior year, he caught 52 passes for 1208 yards and eight touchdowns for the Bellaire football team.

    Besides football, he was also an All-Ohio choice in basketball and won the state championship in both the 100 and 200-meter dash in track.

    He ended his college career fourth in both receptions with 108 and receiving yards with 1,894, and second in touchdown receptions with 19.

    Joey Galloway’s Early Career As NFL Player

    Talking about his professional career, he started as an American football player at the age of 24, joining the Seattle Seahawks in 1995. He played for five seasons, and the club sold him to Dallas Cowboys in 2000.

    After playing 48 games for the team, he was traded to Tampa Bay Buccaneers in 2004. However, he suffered an injury and was inactive for the first six games of the season.

    In 2009, he was released from Tampa Bay Buccaneers and joined New England Patriots as a free agent. Joey, who stands at the height of 5 feet 11 inches (1.8 meters), was associated with Pittsburgh Steelers and Washington Redskins at the end of his professional football career.

    Joey's Post Football Career On ESPN; What Is He Doing Now?

    After the end of his football career in 2010, Joey joined ESPN as a college football analyst at the start of the 2011 college football season.

    He used to appear as a game analyst alongside play-by-play commentator Beth Mowins and reporter Paul Carcaterra. He later came to the ESPN studio analyzing the college football games.

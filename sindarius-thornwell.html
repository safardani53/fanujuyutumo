    Sindarius Thornwell | Career

    High School Career

    Sindarius averaged 26.8 points per game and averaged 8.6 rebounds and 3.6 assists as well. He led the Oak Hill Academy team to a 33-5 record during his senior season.

    ESPN.com ranked him as the 39th best player, and Rivals.com and 247sport.com ranked him a 4-star recruit. Sindarius was also ranked as the 11th best shooting guard in his class and 43 overall prospects by Rivals.com.

    ESPN.com rated him the number 39 overall prospect in the 2013 class. He was the top-rated player in the state of South Carolina. Sindarius competed in the 2012 Nike Global Challenge, playing in 4 games.

    College Career

    Frank Martin coached him at the University of South Carolina.

    2013-14

    Sindarius started all 34 games of his rookie campaign. During his freshman year, he averaged 13.4 points, 4.1 rebounds, 3.0 assists, and 1.2 steals in 29.6 minutes per game.

    His score of 13.4 points per game was ranked second among freshmen in the SEC in overall games, while 1.2 steals per game was ranked second, and 3.0 assists per game were ranked third.

    Sindarius averaged 15.6 points, 4.7 rebounds, 4.0 assists, and 1.2 steals in 33.2 minutes per game in SEC games. He scored the first 20-point game of his career at No. 23 Baylor on November 12.

    He also played a then-season high 36 minutes against Manhattan on December 17. Sindarius posted 25 points on career-best nine field goals against LSU on January 11.

    On January 18, he posted his first career double-double against Ole Miss with 24 points and a season-high 11 rebounds. Also, Sindarius had season-best four 3-point field goals in 37 minutes against the Rebels.

    On January 22, he scored a career-high 26 points on 8-of-12 shooting, including nine made free throws, against the University of Georgia.

    Besides, on February 15, he scored a team-high 22 points with four rebounds and four assists against Alabama. He was named a member of the All-SEC Freshman Team.

    2014-15

    Sindarius started all 33 games of the season and averaged 11.1 points, 4.9 rebounds, 2.2 assists, and a team-high 1.3 steals in 30.6 minutes per game.

    He averaged 10.6 points in SEC games, 4.2 rebounds, 2.2 assists, and 1.1 steals in 32.1 minutes per game.

    He recorded a team-high 23 double-figure scoring games, one double-double, 17 games with at least five rebounds, and 12 games with at least three assists.

    Sindarius ranked 10th in the SEC in steals per game, which was 1.3. He recorded a career-high of six steals in a win against UNC Asheville on November 26.

    2015-16

    Sindarius started all 34 games of the season and averaged 13.4 points, 4.8 rebounds, 3.8 assists, and 1.5 steals in 33.1 minutes per game. He averaged 14.3 points in SEC games, 4.7 rebounds, 3.9 assists, and 1.4 steals in 35.2 minutes per game.

    He ranked sixth in the SEC in both steals per game (1.5) and minutes per game (33.1) in overall games. Against Clemson, his first-half dunk was ESPN Sportscenter’s number 1 play of the day.

    On January 2, he scored a team-high of 18 points in a win against Memphis. He collected his 1000th career point in a win against the Commodores.

    2016-17

    His senior year is when Sindarius truly shined. He averaged 21.4 points, 7.2 rebounds, and 2.2 steals in 34.2 minutes of action per game, which were career-highs in nearly every statistical category, along with 44.4 field goal percent and 83.0 free throw percent.

    He was named the SEC Player of the Year by the league’s coaches; also named All-SEC First Team and the All-Defensive Team.

    In SEC, Sindarius averaged a high of 21.4 points and 2.1 steals per game. His 66 steals during the season rank tied for seventh all-time in Carolina history for a single season.

    The basketballer’s 663 points score rank fourth all-time in Carolina history for a single season. He recorded a team-high of 21 points in a win over Michigan on November 23.

    Besides, he set career-high 44 points, 21 rebounds, and 56 minutes in a game against Alabama on February 7. On March 26, he was named East Regional Most Outstanding Player.

    Sindarius was the main leader to help the No. 7 seeded South Carolina proceed to the Final Four in the 2017 NCAA Tournament, which was the highest result in South Carolina’s history.

    Besides that, his quote “We in it, Why not win it?” following a win was quite famous. It showed his winner’s attitude.

    Professional Career

    Los Angeles Clippers (2017-2019)

    Originally, Sindarius was selected with the 48th pick in the 2017 NBA draft on June 22, 2017, by the Milwaukee Bucks. However, his draft rights were traded to the Los Angeles Clippers. He signed on with the Los Angeles Clippers on July 26.

    Rio Grande Valley Vipers

    Sindarius signed with the Cleveland Cavaliers on August 4, 2019, and played in three preseason games. However, he was waived on October 16.

    The Canton Charge acquired him, but he was traded to the Rio Grande Valley Vipers on October 26, 2019, for a first-round G League pick in 2020. In the 2019-20 season, he averaged 9.2 points, 5.2 rebounds, and 4.6 assists per game on 42.9 percent shooting.

    New Orleans Pelicans

    He was signed by the New Orleans Pelicans on July 6, 2020, as a substitute player for the remainder of the 2019-20 season.

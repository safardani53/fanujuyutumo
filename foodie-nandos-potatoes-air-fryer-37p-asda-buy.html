    26 Mar 2024, 00:00

    A FOODIE has shared the recipe for her DIY Nando’s Air Fryer potatoes – and says they’re one of the best things she’s ever tasted.

    But there’s even better news, as the mum said the tasty side dish costs just 37p thanks to Asda, and you can easily make them too. 

    Sammy took to her TikTok profile @sam_xtx to shout about her latest culinary creation.

    She explained that she’d picked up some of Asda’s Just Essential New Potatoes, which come in the tin with their now trademark yellow packaging and cost just 37p. 

    She then decided to get inventive in the kitchen and see what she could rustle up with them.

    So she dug around in her cupboards to find the other ingredients and gave it a whirl. 

    Read More in Air Fryer

    Sammy explained that the first step was to drain any excess liquid from the tin and then cut them all in half to make bite size portions.

    Next, she placed them in a bowl and added her seasoning – a generous pouring of olive oil, some garlic powder and some salt.

    And, of course, to give them that delicious peri-peri flavouring it’s time to reach for the Nando’s Peri Peri salt, which can be bought in store or from certain supermarkets, including Tesco and Asda. 

    Once all the extra ingredients were in the bowl, Sammy gave them a good stir to make sure that they were evenly covered.

    Most read in Fabulous

    Then, it’s as simple as popping them into the Air Fryer and letting them cook. 

    And when Sammy got them out, she was left with perfectly brown and crispy spuds that would rival roast potatoes. 

    Alongside the video tutorial, Sammy wrote: “These were UNREAL.

    “I just threw it all in a bowl, gave it a mix and Air Fried for approximately 30 mins at 200.

    She also dished out another tip for fellow foodies to add even more flavour, as she revealed: “I added extra Peri Peri before throwing in the Air Fryer.”

    And the home cook said they were even better than she had anticipated, as she wrote: “They were honestly amazing. I wasn’t expecting them to be so good!”

    Meanwhile, her social media followers were so impressed by the easy method and how good they looked that they said they’d be making them as soon as they could.

    While others said they were already a fan of the Asda tinned potatoes, so couldn’t wait to sample them with the Nando’s style twist.

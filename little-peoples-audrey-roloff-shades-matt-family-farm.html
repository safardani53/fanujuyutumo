    AUDREY Roloff has decided to take an important item from the Roloff family farm, but she needs help to do it.

    Audrey, 31, and her husband Jeremy Roloff, 32, got married almost nine years ago when their friend gifted them a unique present.

    The newlyweds received an apple tree as a present from their close friend.

    That tree was planted on Roloff Farms, but now the TLC star wants to move her tree, and she enlisted her followers to help.

    On her Instagram Story, she posted a picture of the beautiful blossoming tree.

    She wrote, "This is our apple tree that was given to us as a wedding gift by my best friend, and it's now almost nine years old.

    "We planted it on Roloff Farms after we got married. We are trying to figure out if there is a way to transport it to our property without shocking it too much.

    "If you have experience moving apple trees send me a DM."

    The tree needs to be moved because Matt Roloff, 61, decided to sell the family farm - and the tree along with it.

    THE SHADE IS REAL

    Audrey and Jeremy also shaded his father, Matt, in a recent video after refusing to visit the family farm.

    The couple was enjoying a getaway at Bolt Farm Treehouse in Whitwell, Tennessee, and have been documenting their stay on social media.

    Audrey took to her Instagram Stories to first share a photo of the stunning view from their bed at the resort.

    The room appeared to be inside a dome overlooking the mountains and beautiful landscape.

    She simply captioned the post: "Morning," with a heart eyes emoji.

    Jeremy took hold of the camera in the following post by filming his wife cooking breakfast in their full-service kitchen.

    He then walked toward the window to show the rainy weather outside the dome.

    The LPBW alum joked that they were experiencing some "Oregon weather" as he zoomed in on the raindrops on the window.

    However, Jeremy and Audrey weren't bothered by the conditions, commenting that they enjoyed waking up to the sound of the rain.

    Audrey gave her followers a closer look at the breathtaking sights from their deck in the next video, showcasing the cloudy skies and acres of land surrounding them.

    She played the country song, Hell Of A View, by Eric Church in the background to explain her feelings about their vacation spot.

    The mom of three again gushed over the view in the following clip capturing her and Jeremy standing on the deck.

    "What a view!" Audrey yelled at the camera.

    Jeremy echoed the thought, joking: "We know a thing or two about a view," which his wife repeated in her caption.

    The pair's enthusiasm seemed to be a jab at Jeremy's father as things have been tense since the patriarch turned down his son's offer to purchase the family farm last year.

    END OF AN ERA

    Matt's trademark for Roloff Farms has expired, as the business name was dissolved ahead of pumpkin season, The U.S. Sun exclusively revealed.

    Matt owns and runs Roloff Farms - a 143-acre property known for its famous pumpkin season - in Hillsboro, Oregon.

    He had two trademarks for Roloff Farms, which were filed in 2014. 

    The U.S. Sun can exclusively reveal both trademarks expired in 2021 because Matt did not file to renew them.

    The trademarks covered, according to the United States Patent and Trademark Office: “Educational services, namely, providing courses of instruction in the fields of agriculture, fruits, vegetables, farming, and animals; providing information in the field of wedding planning and wedding parties.

    “Organizing community-supported agricultural cultural programs and community-shared agricultural cultural events; conducting guided tours of a farm featuring pumpkin picking; arranging and conducting educational farm tours."

    They also covered: “Organizing community fall festivals featuring a variety of activities, namely picking of vegetables and providing wagon tours and pony rides; conducting guided educational tours for groups in pumpkin patches, vegetable fields, conducting guided farm tours featuring pick it yourself and pick your own fruits and vegetables."

    Matt has filed no new trademark applications since these two expired.

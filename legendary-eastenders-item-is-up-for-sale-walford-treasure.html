    WIN a one-of-a-kind 100% authentic BBC prop this year for Red Nose Day.

    Ever wanted to look as hard as Luther, as en vogue as Villanelle with the glam of Pat Butcher?

    Look no further because this Red Nose Day has collected a trove of iconic telly treasures that you can get your hands on. 

    Simply browse, select your dream item and you could be in with a chance to win one these 100% genuine precious BBC props.

    This year Red Nose Day is set to be sweeter than ever, giving the Red Nose Nation the opportunity to win these incredible objects from some of the BBC’s best known programmes, including EastEnders, Waterloo Road, Killing Eve, The Apprentice: You’re Fired and more.

    Items up for grabs include Pat Butcher’s earrings, Luther’s tie and coat, Craig’s Strictly ‘10’ paddle, a cloak from The Traitors and much more.

    The earring collection from the BBC soap is the proverbial kitchen sink of vintage glam.

    They are a show stopping, eye-glaring and breathtaking bejewelled statement piece.

    Or drape yourself in deception morning, noon and night with The Traitors cloak.

    It is the perfect get-up to beguile and betray friends, family and work colleagues.

    Or opt for a Pointless winner trophy and show people you're clever without even going onto the show.

    This is both a mantle piece maker and office desk powerplay rolled into one.

    Other items include a Waterloo Road school jumper, tie and blazer, Casualty's Ethan’s scrubs, stethoscope and nametag, RuPaul’s Drag Race UK's Brit crew pants and judges badge, a mug from Morning Live and much more.

    The initiative was announced in a trail fronted by Sharon Horgan and Lenny Rush, who revealed some of the amazing objects on offer. 

    It is completely free to enter, however entrants can choose to make a donation to Comic Relief if they wish, which will help make a life changing difference to people facing the toughest time of their lives.

    Katie Taylor, Commissioning Editor, BBC Entertainment, said: “This is a fantastic chance for Telly Treasure hunters to win and own a bit of BBC TV magic and to help raise awareness of Comic Relief which changes the lives of people for the better”.

    For the very first time, AJ Odudu and Joel Dommett will host the Red Nose Day night of TV alongside Zoe Ball, Paddy McGuinness and David Tennant.

    Returning to screens live on BBC One on Friday 17 March, they will be joined by a host of stars and supporters to bring viewers an unmissable evening of comedy, live music, exclusive prizes and plenty of surprises.

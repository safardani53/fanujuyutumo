    WAKE UP FOR SALES

    JCPenney joins the list of retailers closed for business on Turkey Day, including unexpected staples like Target and Walmart.

    However, JCPenney followed up the holiday closure with the news of sales beginning early on Black Friday.

    The U.S. Sun has confirmed with a store associate all locations will welcome shoppers starting promptly at 5 am.

    However, as the tale of Black Friday shopping goes, many Americans will most likely show up beforehand to get first dibs.

    read more on black friday

    To give you a big picture, this year, it's estimated that 132 million Americans plan to shop the pre-holiday sales (such as Black Friday and Cyber Monday), according to the latest research from Finder.

    The average person will spend a pretty $708 on deals totaling roughly $60 billion of bargains.

    Many retailers are counting on these profits to stay afloat, especially after nationwide mass store closures and bankruptcies.

    JCPENNEY INVESTS $1 BILLION IN STORES

    JCPenney announced plans to spend $1 billion to make store improvements by the end of 2025.

    Most read in Money

    Revitalization efforts come after the 121-year-old company filed for bankruptcy in 2020.

    JCPenney is using the money to remodel stores, upgrade its online shopping site and app, and make its supply network more efficient so that online orders are delivered quicker.

    The investment will also employ more people, as the chain plans to implement walking cashiers.

    Some store employees will have mobile devices to scan inventory and ring up transactions.

    Of course, this is all in an effort to place JCPenney back on the map to compete with other major retailers.

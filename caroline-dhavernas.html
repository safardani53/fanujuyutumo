    Who Is Caroline Dhavernas? Age And Biography

    Caroline Dhavernas was born on May 15, 1978, in Montreal, Quebec, Canada. She is the daughter of actors Michèle Deslauriers and Sébastien Dhavernas and has an actress sister named Gabrielle. She is 45 years old as of 2021. Caroline, a French-Canadian, began learning English at a young age in the English-speaking elementary The Priory School.

    How Did Caroline Dhavernas Pursue Her Career? Professional Life

    Caroline Dhavernas made her television debut in 1990 in an episode of “Les Filles de Caleb,” and two years later, she made her film debut in Michel Langlois’ drama “Cap Tourmente” starring Andrée Lachapelle and Roy Dupuis. Dhavernas began her career in 1997 as Roxanne Roy in eight episodes of the television series “Lobby,” and she finished the decade with roles in films such as “Running Home” and “L’île de sable,” both released in 1999.

    Caroline appeared in the drama “Lost and Delirious” alongside Piper Perabo, Jessica Paré, and Mischa Barton in 2001, and the same year played the title role in the biography “Heart: The Marilyn Bell Story,” about a Canadian teenager who was the first person to swim across Lake Ontario in 1954; this role helped her secure more roles in the following years. Caroline had the lead role in Michael MacKenzie’s drama “The Baroness and the Pig” (2002), co-starring Patricia Clarkson and Colm Feore, and she also played Anna in the sports comedy “Out Cold” starring Flex Alexander, A.J. Cook, and David Denman. She then appeared in an episode of the Golden Globe-nominated series “Law & Order” (2002), but her biggest success came in the 2004 series “Wonderfalls,” where she played Jaye Tyler for 14 episodes.

    More On Career

    Following this success, Dhavernas shifted her focus to films, where she spent the next several years – in 2006, she played a supporting role in the Golden Globe Award-nominated film “Hollywoodland,” starring Adrien Brody, Ben Affleck, Diane Lane, and Bob Hoskins, and in 2007, Caroline worked with Chris Cooper, Ryan Phillippe, and Dennis Haysbert in Billy Ray’s “Breach.” Dhavernas had appeared in films such as “Passchendaele” (2008), “The Cry of the Owl,” starring Paddy Considine and Julia Stiles, and “Father and Guns,” both in 2009.

    Caroline Dhavernas did not appear on television for six years following “Wonderfalls,” but she returned in an episode of “Law & Order: Criminal Intent” in 2010 before appearing in the Golden Globes Award-nominated miniseries “The Pacific” (2010). Despite this, she continued to work in films, including a supporting role in Jennifer Aniston’s romantic comedy “The Switch” (2010), starring Jennifer Aniston, Jason Bateman, Patrick Wilson, Jeff Goldblum, and Juliette Lewis. Caroline had a busy year, as she also appeared in the horror film “Devil” with Chris Messina and Bokeem Woodbine, as well as in “Wrecked” with Adrien Brody. Dhavernas portrayed Dr. Lily Brenner in 13 episodes of the series “Off the Map” in 2011 and Dr. Alana Bloom in 39 episodes of the Primetime Emmy Award-nominated “Hannibal” between 2013 and 2015, alongside Mads Mikkelsen, Hugh Dancy, Laurence Fishburne, and Gillian Anderson.

    Bruce William Devlin is an Australian professional golfer, sportsman and golf course designer. Devlin was born in Armidale, Australia. He turned professional in 1961 and joined the PGA Tour in 1962 after an amateur career in Australia, including a victory at the Australian Amateur in 1959. During his career on the PGA Tour he scored eight victories, all occurring from 1964 to 1972. In 1972, he earned $ 119,768 and ranked eighth on the monetization list. In the Senior PGA Tour, Devlin won a tournament, the 1995 FHP Health Care Classic. At the end of the 1998 golf season, Devlin decided to retire from the Senior PGA Tour to focus on the business of architecture and design. golf course and its commitment to the golf broadcasts of ESPN. The main focus of Devlin's career over the past 30 years has been his work as an architect and golf course designer. Devlin has designed and built more than 150 golf courses around the world, including Australia, Japan, Scotland, the Bahamas and the United States.

    When it comes to style, what you wear under your clothing can matter just as much as your clothing itself. This couldn’t be more true of bras, since straps and materials can easily get in the way and distract from the finished look — which is why we can’t imagine our dresser drawers without a strapless bra. “A strapless bra is meant to support the breasts while you’re wearing strapless clothing or clothing such as tube tops, strapless dresses, and spaghetti strap tops that has unique straps that may show with a traditional bra,” Annette Bourne, a stylist and lingerie fit specialist with over 20 years of experience, tells PEOPLE.

With this in mind, we decided the only way to find the best strapless bras is to try them on and conduct real-world testing — so that’s exactly what we did.

    Things to Consider Before Buying Strapless Bras

    Material

    When shopping for strapless bras, it always helps to consider the different types of materials. “The stronger the material, the more support and lift you get,” says Bourne. She explains that, sometimes, softer materials can squish the breasts together (especially in a strapless style), and as a result, can impact the overall shape of the breasts. 

    In addition to support, Luciani Sena says the material matters if you are looking for something specific. She says, for example, those who tend to sweat in the bust area might want something more breathable like cotton or opt for a sweat-wicking material while those who want a completely smooth look might prefer a foam cup and microfiber material, such as the Intimissimi Giada Microfiber Bandeau Bra or the Spanx Up For Anything Strapless Bra. 

    Grip

    One of the most important aspects of a strapless bra is the grip — without it, a bra could easily slip and slide down your back which is the exact opposite of what you want. When considering which strapless bra to purchase, Luciana Sena says to check for “extra features to help it grip to the body like silicone around the inner sides of the band.” Band size also helps ensure that a strapless bra fits snugly (yet still comfortable) under the busts and a wider band can also add an additional layer of grip and support.

    One of the best strapless bras with grip is the Spanx Up For Anything Strapless Bra, which is a runner-up for our best overall category. This bra features Spanx SmartGrip technology and With Me tape on the bra band so that the strapless bra stays in place and never slips. 

    Shape

    According to Bourne, shape also matters for a more seamless effect. With this in mind, she says to ensure a bra doesn’t sit above a shirt or dress’ neckline. She also says to avoid options that are too far below the neckline “because then you’ll see the top of the bra cup through the shirt.” If you’re shopping for a specific occasion, it helps to try on the strapless bra under the garment you plan to wear, that way you can get a good idea of how it looks underneath.

    Versatility 

    For a more cost-effective option, Bourne says to go for something more versatile. “If you need a strapless bra, try one with removable straps that can be used in many ways,” she explains, noting that a convertible bra can go from strapless to one strap to racerback, to traditional tank straps, giving you multiple uses in one bra. 

    After testing dozens of options, we found that the best strapless bra with convertible straps is the Wacoal Women's Red Carpet Strapless Bra, which has comfortable straps that can easily transform into a range of bra styles. Our top pick for the best strapless bra overall, the Aerie Real Sunnie Strapless Lightly Lined Bra, also features removable straps which are easily manipulated depending on your styling needs. The Cosabella Forte Convertible Strapless Bra, is another excellent choice for a strapless bra with removable straps, plus we love the lace detail and color options.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    • How do you keep a strapless bra from falling down?

      There’s nothing more annoying than a strapless bra that keeps sliding down. To prevent this from happening, Bourne says it’s essential to wear the correct size. “It’s best to get measured and get something that fits around the ribcage properly,” she explains. Our top pick for the best size range is the Thirdlove 24/7 Classic Strapless Bra because it not only has impressive cup sizes but also comes in a band size range of 32 to 44, making it easier to find an option that fits around the ribcage. 


      Depending on the bra and your band and bust size, some bras can still slide around. Luciana Sena says the trick to keeping a strapless bra in place is to “go down a band size and also look for silicone lining the inner side of the band.” She also recommends staying away from lotions and oils on the chest and ribcage as this can lubricate the skin and cause even the best bras with grips to slip.

    • What’s the difference between a strapless bra and a bandeau?

      “A bandeau is generally soft, has no wire, and is something you casually slip on — it doesn’t offer shape or lift and it’s normally used for comfort,” says Bourne. While a bandeau is technically a type of strapless bra, they’re also popular undershirt options for those who want some coverage but don’t want to wear a full camisole or tank top. Additionally, Bourne says some people might even use strapless bras under bandeaus because they like the look of it but still want the shape and support from a strapless bra.

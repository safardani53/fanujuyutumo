    A FAMILY has shared how they spent just $6,000 to prepare their tiny house.

    Tiny houses have proven to be an affordable option for those looking to downsize.

    TikTokers John and Fin live together in Georgia in a 12x30 shed they transformed into a spacious tiny home 11 years ago.

    The home features a large living and kitchen space, two lofted areas, a ton of storage space, and a greenhouse bathroom.

    On their account (@BelovedCabin), they detail their daily lives in the house and share how they were able to put the whole thing together.

    In one of their most recent videos, they took viewers through a home tour and broke down the costs of the renovation.

    read more on tiny homes

    They spent $6,500 on the shed, and another $6,000 to complete the renovations inside.

    The home features two separate areas for their pets, a "catio," and a dog run.

    Several large windows let in natural light, and intricate hanging light fixtures keep things bright at night while adding a chic look.

    There is a spacious living area with a DIY sectional they made for only $200.

    Most read in Money

    In the kitchen, there is a spacious commercial sink that was only $100 on Craigslist.

    The long table in the room can double as counter space when cooking and prepping meals.

    The pricier additions to the home included the breathtaking greenhouse bathroom, complete with a chandelier and clawfoot tub, totaling $1,500.

    John and Fin live off the grid and rely on solar power, which was another $1,500.

    ANOTHER SMALL OPTION

    While it's incredible and extremely customized, if you're looking for something a bit more simple than John and Fin's place, there are options for you too.

    You can even order a tiny home from Amazon and have it delivered right to your door.

    Prices start at under $3,000 and they qualify for Amazon Prime benefits, guaranteeing delivery in three to seven days.

    These homes can take less than a day to build depending on size and construction experience.

    To make it even easier, the home kits come with pre-assembled doors and pre-cut ready-to-assemble wood.

    The insides of the homes are left plain so you can decorate the space to your liking.

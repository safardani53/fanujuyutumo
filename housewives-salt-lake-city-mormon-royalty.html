    Real Housewives of Salt Lake City: What is Mormon royalty?

    A FEW cast members of the Real Housewives of Salt Lake City may belong to a higher echelon family tree. Lisa Barlow and Heather Gay, two of the six cast members on the Real Housewives of Salt Lake City, belong to Mormon royalty.

    08 Jun 2024, 00:00

    A FEW cast members of the Real Housewives of Salt Lake City may belong to a higher echelon family tree.

    Lisa Barlow and Heather Gay, two of the six cast members on the Real Housewives of Salt Lake City, belong to Mormon royalty.

    What is the Real Housewives of Salt Lake City?

    The Real Housewives of Salt Lake City takes place in the mountains of Utah.

    The hidden social circle is made up of successful women who have created their own paradise filled with luxury homes, shopping sprees and multi-million-dollar businesses and brands.

    What is Mormon royalty?

    According to a Reddit user, Mormon royalty is "families of high ranking former or current leaders of the church.

    "Probably a grandson of one of the prophets or 12 apostles," wrote the user.

    Which cast members belong to Mormon royalty?

    Lisa Barlow's family converted to the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints when she was a child and was raised as a practicing Mormon.

    Barlow considers herself  "Mormon 2.0" as she is not one to "adhere to all of the traditional and strict Mormon rules," according to her Bravo profile.

    "When you think about the Mormon church there's like a laundry list of things that they don't ever do. Which basically makes you perfect, and I am far from perfect," Barlow said during an interview with Us Weekly.

    "I just do Mormonism my way."

    Heather was once a "devout Mormon from birth," although things have now changed.

    She was married to "Mormon royalty" for the past 11 years, but distanced herself from the church after her divorce.

    “It all came crashing down when we got divorced five years ago,” she said on the show.

    “In the Mormon church, divorce is not an option.”

    “I didn’t know anyone that was Mormon and divorced and single and happy," she told Page Six after her split.

    "I either had to get remarried or redefine my entire life. I had three little kids and I didn’t know how to do it and nobody gave me any guidance.”

    She now says she's a "good Mormon gone bad" and says doesn't practice as much as she once did.

    Who are the cast members of the Real Housewives of Salt Lake City?

    There are six cast members to the Real Housewives of Salt Lake City.

    Lisa Barlow, Mary Cosby, Heather Gay, Meredith Marks, Whitney Rose and Jen Shah.

    Who is the wealthiest family in the Real Housewives of Salt Lake City?

    Barlow and Cosby top the list of wealthiest RHOSLC cast members with a net worth of $5million each.

    In November 2020, Barlow gave viewers a tour of her minimalist Utah home, admitting that she is a fan of "simple, very clean, long lines."

    Gay, who was once Mormon royalty, co-owns a cosmetic medical practice called Beauty Lab and Laser.

    She also co-hosts the Live Love Lab podcast.

    The company has helped her build an estimated $1.7 million net worth.

    Marks is a jewelry designer and is already mixed in celebrity circles, as she makes trinkets for celebs from Rihanna to Hannah Brown.

    Her designs are featured in Neiman Marcus stores.

    She has also worked in real estate as well as invested in a health club.

    She is worth an estimated $1 million.

    Rose left the Mormon church around 2010 after falling in love with her boss, with whom she was having an affair.

    The pair divorced their spouses and have been married for ten years.

    She owns a skincare line called Iris and Beau and boasts almost 16,000 followers on Instagram.

    Rose is worth $3million according to Cheat Sheet.

    Shah is of Tongan and Hawaiian descent and grew up in Salt Lake City. 

    Shah grew up Mormon but converted to Islam when she learned about the historical mistreatment of black people in her previous religion.

    She is married to Sharrieff Shah, a football coach at the University of Utah.
